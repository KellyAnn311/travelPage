Travel Safety Tips

Do Your Research

Get to know your destination in depth before you arrive. Read traveler reviews and consult with locals for information about the safest neighborhoods, places to stay and incidences of crime. Always be aware of your surroundings and never leave your food or drink unattended.

Blend With The Crowd

People who look like they’re from out of town are especially vulnerable to crime, so try to blend in as much as you can. Choose inconspicuous clothing that won't attract attention. Be discreet when looking at maps and approach people carefully if you need to ask for directions.

Important Documents

You never know when you might need a copy of your passport, driver’s license or another form of identification. Scan these documents to save online and print out several hard copies. That way, you won’t be scrambling to find proper documentation if you need to get home.

Antigua Safety

Tropical beaches and English-speaking natives make Antigua a prime vacation location for U.S. tourists looking for a relatively safe, comfortable island vacation. Street crime and thefts occur on the island, although these types of crimes can affect you anywhere. Keeping an eye on personal possessions and avoiding known dangerous areas can help ensure a trouble-free Antigua vacation.
